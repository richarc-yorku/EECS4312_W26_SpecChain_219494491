# Application: [Calm] 
- Most important difference between pipelines: [The automated pipeline produced specific tests and generic personas, while the manual pipeline produced more negative and similarly themed groups.]
- Most useful pipeline: [The Automatic pipeline seems like a good springboard and source for ideas for developing manual requirements, this allows strong tracing between elements while reducing time spend on theorycrafting] 
- Most surprising finding: [Several automated groups included reviews directly opposing stated views. This may be due to lacking cleaning methods or inherent flaws with the AI]
- Traceability: [The manual pipeline produced the strongest traceability due to focus being put into maintaining it. However, with better prompting it may be possible for automatic pipelines to approach or rival the manual process]
- Observed weakness in the automated pipeline: [Generally, when generating multiple tests for a single requirement, they were all similar and overlapped significantly.
                                                Generated groups and personas often included irrelevant or contrarian reviews that were not relevant to their description.
                                                Turning automatically generated files into hybrid files often took more time than creating manual files from scratch.]
